A heavy strap hinge with two long leaf plates, alternating barrel knuckles, and a prominent hinge pin along one edge. The leaves should feel thick and load-bearing rather than like decorative sheet metal, with the knuckles occupying real mechanical volume. Make it feel real with formed plate edges, machined pin ends, washers, and believable leaf thickness relative to the barrel diameter. One revolute joint connects the two leaves along the barrel axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
