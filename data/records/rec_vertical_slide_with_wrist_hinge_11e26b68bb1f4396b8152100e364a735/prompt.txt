A vertical tool axis with a narrow Z slide, a carried hinge frame at the bottom of the slide, and a compact rectangular end plate mounted in the hinge frame. The slide should read as the main moving mass and the hinge frame as a lighter terminal articulation attached to it. Push the realism with machined guides, hinge caps, stop pads, and believable support overlap through both the lift travel and the hinge sweep. The Z slide is a prismatic stage and the carried end plate is a revolute stage about a supported wrist axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
