A machine-tending arm with a short pedestal base, a tall shoulder fork, a deep first link, a slimmer second link, and a compact cylindrical wrist nose. The arm should clearly show which parts are fixed supports and which parts are moving links, with no ambiguity at the joint roots. Aim for believable industrial realism with machined trunnion cheeks, inspection covers, flange rings, and carefully proportioned link thicknesses. The shoulder and elbow are serial revolute joints about parallel cross-axes, and the wrist is an independent revolute joint about the local tool axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
