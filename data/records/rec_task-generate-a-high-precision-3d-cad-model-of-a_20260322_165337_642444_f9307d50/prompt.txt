 Task: Generate a high-precision 3D CAD model of a standard Deep Groove Ball Bearing assembly.

Structural Components & Geometry:

Outer Ring: A hollow cylinder with a precision-machined internal concave raceway. The outer diameter (D) and width (B) adhere to ISO metric standards.

Inner Ring: A hollow cylinder positioned concentrically inside the outer ring, featuring an external concave raceway aligned with the outer ring's raceway.

Rolling Elements: A set of high-precision spherical balls (standard quantity based on pitch diameter). Each ball is tangentially constrained within the void formed by the inner and outer raceways, maintaining point contact.

Cage (Retainer)：A two-piece ribbon-type or riveted cage structure situated between the inner and outer rings. It features spherical pockets to encapsulate and equidistantly space the rolling elements along the pitch circle.

Mechanical Constraints & Kinematics:

Coaxial Alignment: The Z-axis is the primary axis of rotation for all components.

Degrees of Freedom (DOF): The Inner Ring has 1-DOF rotation relative to the Outer Ring around the Z-axis.

Rolling Constraint: Rolling elements are constrained to revolve around the bearing center while simultaneously rotating around their individual axes.

Clearance: Incorporate standard radial and axial internal clearances (C3 fit logic) between the balls and the raceways.

Technical Specifications: All edges should feature structural fillets (radii) for stress distribution. The assembly must maintain interference-free geometry between the cage and the rings.
