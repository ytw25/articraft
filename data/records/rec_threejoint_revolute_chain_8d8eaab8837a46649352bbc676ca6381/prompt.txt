A wall-mounted inspection arm with a grounded base plate, three rigid links, and a small square pad at the end. The link lengths should vary enough to give the arm a clear shoulder, elbow, and wrist silhouette rather than a uniform ladder of bars. Make it feel real with machined hinge housings, formed link edges, local ribs, and believable thickness around each revolute joint. The three articulations are revolute joints about parallel hinge axes in one plane. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
