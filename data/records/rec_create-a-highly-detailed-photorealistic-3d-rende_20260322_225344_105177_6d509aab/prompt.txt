1. The Base Jaw (Lower Leaf):

Geometry: An elongated, slightly convex rectangular strip of stamped steel. The rear (tail) is flat, while the front terminates in a concave, crescent-shaped, upward-facing beveled cutting edge.

Features: A smooth, circular hole is drilled vertically near the front, just behind the cutting edge, to accommodate the pivot pin.

Mechanics: Acts as the rigid foundation and bottom cutting surface.

Geometry: Similar in footprint to the base jaw but angled slightly upward from the rear joint. It terminates at the front with a downward-facing concave, crescent-shaped beveled cutting edge that perfectly aligns with the bottom edge.

Features: Welded or firmly riveted to the base jaw at the rear. It also features a vertical hole near the front, perfectly concentric with the hole in the base jaw.

Mechanics: Acts as a cantilever spring. Its natural resting state is biased upward to keep the jaws open. It flexes downward when pressure is applied.

Geometry: A vertical, cylindrical metal rod passing through the holes of both jaws.

Features: The bottom has a flat retaining flange (or hook) that anchors under the base jaw. The top features a precisely cut lateral notch (or cross-hole) that sits just above the top jaw.

Mechanics: Holds the entire assembly together and acts as the tension anchor against the upward force of the lever.

4. The Lever (Handle):

Geometry: An elongated, ergonomic metal arm with a slight depression along its top surface for thumb placement.

Features: The front engagement point features a slot or transverse bar that hooks directly into the notch at the top of the pivot pin.

Mechanics (The Fulcrum/Cam): The underside of the lever, immediately adjacent to the pin connection, features a distinct, rounded cam (a raised fulcrum bump). When the lever is rotated 180 degrees into the active position and pressed downward, this cam presses against the flat top surface of the top jaw. Because the pin acts as a rigid vertical anchor, the downward rotation of the lever forces the cam to push the top jaw down, overcoming the spring tension and shearing the cutting edges together.
