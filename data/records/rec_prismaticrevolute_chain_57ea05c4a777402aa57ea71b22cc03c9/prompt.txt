A drawer-like shuttle with a side-mounted pivot block and a small inspection paddle at the end of a short link. The sliding base and the hinged link should have clearly separate support structures so the articulation stack is immediately legible. Make it feel real with machined guide rails, carriage flanges, shoulder bolts, and practical clearances around the hinge block and the moving paddle. The shuttle is a prismatic joint along its guide axis and the paddle link is a revolute joint on the shuttle. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
