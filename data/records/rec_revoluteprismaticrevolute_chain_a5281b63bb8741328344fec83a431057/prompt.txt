A side-hinged inspection boom with a compact root pivot, a sliding center extension, and a small tilting head plate at the end. The mechanism should feel like a hinged support arm that gains reach through the center stage rather than through extra link count. Use realistic detail such as trunnion cheeks, slider covers, wrist caps, fastener arrays, and practical hard stops at the root and tip. The root is a revolute joint on the mounting bracket, the center stage is prismatic along the arm, and the head plate is a distal revolute joint on a cross-pin. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
