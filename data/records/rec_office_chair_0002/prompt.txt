A high-back executive office chair in a refined corporate variant with a tall padded backrest, a sculpted lumbar panel, and a five-point star base. The seat cushion is a broad padded box on a stamped metal seat plate, and the backrest panel attaches at the rear base of the seat plate through a visible recline housing; the backrest tilts on a revolute hinge from this housing with a tilt-lock knob on the right underside. Each of the two T-shaped armrests slides vertically on a prismatic joint through rectangular guide channels recessed into the seat plate sides. The seat height column is a two-stage nested cylinder — outer fixed sleeve on the star base, inner post carrying the seat plate — on a prismatic joint. The five caster legs are rigid ribs radiating from the base hub, each ending in a small twin-wheel caster yoke on a continuous revolute axle. Keep the base wide and stable, the backrest tall relative to the seat, and all moving elements mounted to clearly visible structural members.
