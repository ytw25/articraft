A compact tool wrist with a yaw collar at the root, a pitch yoke in the middle, and a short roll spindle carrying a flange at the nose. The three axis supports should be easy to distinguish by shape and scale so the wrist does not collapse into one dense blob of geometry. Make it feel like real robot hardware with machined collar faces, trunnion caps, spindle cartridges, and believable clearances around the nested moving members. Yaw is a revolute joint about a vertical axis, pitch is a revolute joint about a horizontal cross-axis, and roll is a revolute joint about the tool axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
