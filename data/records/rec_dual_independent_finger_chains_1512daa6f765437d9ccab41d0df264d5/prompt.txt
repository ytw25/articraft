A compact robot pinch hand with a central palm saddle, two slender finger chains, and offset root knuckles that give each finger its own support block. One finger can be longer and straighter while the other is shorter with a hooked tip so the pair feels purpose-built instead of mirrored by default. The assembly should feel production-real with grounded palm mass, machined hinge barrels, replaceable fingertip pads, and clear parting lines where housings meet brackets. Each finger is a serial three-joint revolute chain bending in one plane from a separate base pivot on the palm. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
