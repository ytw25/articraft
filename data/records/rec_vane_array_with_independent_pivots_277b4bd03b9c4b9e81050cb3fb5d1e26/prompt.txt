A louver-style vane array with a rigid outer frame, several parallel slats, and an individual pivot shaft for every slat. The frame, slats, and shaft supports should all occupy real space so the array feels like a mechanically grounded shutter assembly rather than a flat icon. Make it feel real with sheet-metal rails, hinge knuckles, shaft collars, stop tabs, and believable spacing between adjacent vanes. Each vane is an independent revolute joint about its own supported pivot axis within the fixed frame. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
