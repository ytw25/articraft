A business monitor with a boxy display shell, a telescoping stand neck, and a rounded pedestal base, plus a small bank of menu buttons under the lower bezel. the shell stays slightly thicker than an ultrathin entertainment screen so the support geometry reads clearly, and the controls are separate parts instead of one fused panel. The screen tilts on a horizontal hinge at the stand head, the stand swivels continuously on a vertical axis above the base, and the neck slides prismatically for height adjustment, and the menu buttons articulate independently as small prismatic push controls. Small office-monitor scale.
