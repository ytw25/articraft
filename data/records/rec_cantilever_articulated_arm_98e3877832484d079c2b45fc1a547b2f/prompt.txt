A wall-mounted transfer arm with a stout backplate, an angled shoulder bracket, a tapered first link, a deep elbow knuckle, and a square end plate. The wall plate and shoulder package should read as the heaviest part of the assembly, with the forearm and wrist head progressively lighter toward the tip. Use realistic mechanical detailing like bolted split housings, end caps, side ribs, and practical spacing around the knuckle volumes so it feels manufacturable. The shoulder and elbow rotate about supported horizontal pivots from separate knuckle blocks, and the end plate sits on its own wrist revolute axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
