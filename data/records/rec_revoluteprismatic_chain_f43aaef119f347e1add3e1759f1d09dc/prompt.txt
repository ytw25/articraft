A pedestal-mounted arm with a rotary root joint, a straight beam link, and a telescoping nose slide ending in a flat tool pad. The beam should feel like a rigid carrier for the sliding nose rather than a second hinged link, with the slide visibly guided inside the beam body. Make it read as real hardware with a machined root housing, beam end caps, guide sleeves, and practical overlap between the fixed beam and moving nose stage. The root joint is a revolute axis on the pedestal, and the distal nose is a prismatic slide along the beam axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
