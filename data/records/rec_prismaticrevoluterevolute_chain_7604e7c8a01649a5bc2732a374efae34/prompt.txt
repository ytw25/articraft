A bench module with a base rail, a sliding carriage, a shoulder block on the carriage, and a two-link arm ending in a pad. The carriage should be long enough to read as its own positioning stage before the arm begins, with the arm clearly mounted to the carried shoulder block. Make it read as real hardware with machined rail mounts, joint caps, local ribs, and believable spacing between the carriage body and the hinged links. The carriage is a prismatic joint along the base, the shoulder is a revolute joint on the carriage, and the elbow is a second revolute joint farther out on the arm. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
