A bench manipulator with a grounded shoulder block, an upper link, a forearm link, and a telescoping probe housed in the forearm nose. The sliding probe should read as a separate final stage rather than just a long tip, with the forearm clearly acting as its guide body. Make it feel real with machined hinge housings, ribbed links, guide sleeves, shoulder collars, and practical clearances through the fold path and extension path. Shoulder and elbow are serial revolute joints, and the probe is a prismatic extension along the forearm axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
