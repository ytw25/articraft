Design Syringe as a field-service workhorse. Build it around a graduated barrel with linear plunger rod and nozzle tip. Prioritize chunky hardware, replaceable wear parts, clear maintenance access, and robust tolerances for repeated use. Plunger motion should be linear, coaxial, and bounded by stop geometry. Every part must be connected by real brackets, shafts, hinges, rails, collars, weldments, or housings; absolutely no floating geometry. No floating parts.
