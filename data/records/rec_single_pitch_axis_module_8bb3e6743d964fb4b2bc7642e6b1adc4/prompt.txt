A tilt head with two side towers, a horizontal cross-shaft, and a rectangular payload cradle suspended between the towers. The towers should visibly carry the pivot so the motion reads as a supported tilt mechanism rather than a floating plate. Make it feel real with machined trunnion caps, cheek ribs, clamp faces, and believable structural depth in the side supports. One revolute joint pitches the cradle about the horizontal shaft axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
