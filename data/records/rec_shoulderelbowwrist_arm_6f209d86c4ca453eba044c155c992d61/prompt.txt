A bench robot arm with a rotating shoulder pedestal, an upper arm link, a forearm link, and a compact wrist roll cartridge carrying a tool flange. The pedestal should read as a yaw base while the arm links feel like a classic elbow chain and the wrist reads as a separate terminal module. Make it feel like real industrial hardware with cast joint housings, machined flange faces, service caps, and believable mass progression from base to tip. The shoulder is a revolute joint about a vertical axis, the elbow is a revolute joint about a horizontal arm axis, and the wrist is a revolute joint about the forearm axis. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
