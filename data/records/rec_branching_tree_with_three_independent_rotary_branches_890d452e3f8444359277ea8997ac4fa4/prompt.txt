A three-branch inspection fixture head with a tall rectangular spine, a low side arm, a forward arm, and a high arm ending in three different mounting pads. Each branch is carried from its own collar, trunnion block, or clevis support at a different elevation so the assembly reads as three separate mechanisms tied to one mast. The whole unit should feel like real bench fixturing with machined hubs, welded mast plates, bearing caps, and believable section thickness where the branches load the spine. The lower arm is a revolute joint about a transverse shaft, the forward arm is a revolute joint about a vertical stub axis, and the upper arm is a revolute joint about a longitudinal support shaft. Keep every part mechanically grounded with no floating members, and make sure the articulation paths stay clear so nothing clips through covers, stops, housings, rails, or neighboring links.
